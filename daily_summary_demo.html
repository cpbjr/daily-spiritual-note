Daily Benedictine Reflection

Sunday of the Twenty-fourth Week in Ordinary Time

Today's Readings

First Reading: 1 Timothy 2:1-8
Psalm: Psalm 28:2, 7, 8-9
Gospel: John 19:25-27

Today's readings call us to intercession and faithful discipleship. Paul's letter to Timothy emphasizes prayer for all people, especially those in authority, while the Gospel presents Mary's profound example of faithful presence at the foot of the cross.

Today's Focus: Worship & Community

Sunday calls us to gather in worship and strengthen our bonds of Christian community. The Benedictine tradition teaches us that worship is both personal prayer and communal celebration, creating space for God's presence in our shared life together.

Saint of the Day

Our Lady of Sorrows

Today we honor Mary under the title of Our Lady of Sorrows, contemplating her profound grief at the suffering and death of her Son. This feast invites us to unite our own sorrows with Mary's and to find comfort in her maternal intercession during times of trial.

Reflection & Challenge

In today's readings, we see a call to faithful discipleship even in times of suffering. Mary's presence at the cross teaches us about standing with others in their darkest moments, while Paul's call to prayer reminds us that intercession is a form of love.

Practical Spiritual Challenge: Today, spend time in prayer for someone who is suffering, following Paul's encouragement to intercede for others and Mary's example of faithful presence.

Daily Prayer

Loving God, like Mary at the foot of the cross, help us to remain faithful in times of trial. Grant us the grace to stand with those who suffer and to offer our prayers as instruments of Your peace. May we find in Our Lady of Sorrows a model of trust and surrender to Your will. Amen.
