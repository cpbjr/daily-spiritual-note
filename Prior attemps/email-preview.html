🕊️ Benedictine Reflection: Listening

On this Wednesday, we honor St. Monica, the devoted mother whose persistent prayers and attentive listening to God's voice led to the conversion of her son, St. Augustine. In 1 Thessalonians 2:9-13, St. Paul reminds us of his tireless efforts to proclaim the Gospel, urging the community to live lives worthy of God, who calls us into His kingdom. This echoes the Benedictine theme of listening, as Paul invites the Thessalonians to hear the word of God at work within them, not merely as human words but as divine truth. Conversely, in Matthew 23:27-32, Jesus rebukes the scribes and Pharisees as 'whitewashed tombs'—outwardly righteous but inwardly hypocritical, building monuments to prophets while failing to truly listen to their messages or God's call to authentic holiness.

Today's Challenge

Today, practice Benedictine listening by setting aside 10 minutes for silent prayer, attentively hearing God's voice in Scripture or in a conversation with someone in need, and respond with compassionate action, inspired by St. Monica's perseverance.

🙏 Prayer for Today

Heavenly Father, through the intercession of St. Monica, grant us the grace to listen deeply with the ear of our hearts, as St. Benedict teaches. Help us to receive Your word as Paul proclaimed it, rejecting hypocrisy and embracing true holiness. May our lives reflect Your kingdom. Amen.
